Frequently Asked Questions

Can I really build a home gym for under $500?

Absolutely! Our complete setup costs $487 and covers everything you need for effective HIIT training. You can start with just a mat ($50), jump rope ($30), and resistance bands ($13) for under $100.

What's the most important piece of equipment?

A good yoga mat. You'll use it for every workout - burpees, planks, ab work, stretching. The Manduka PRO is expensive but lasts forever. Budget option: Gaiam Essentials ($25).

Do I need dumbbells AND kettlebells?

Not necessarily. Kettlebells are more versatile for HIIT (swings, snatches, cleans). If you can only afford one, get an adjustable kettlebell. Add dumbbells later for isolation exercises.

What about a pull-up bar?

Great addition if you have $30-50 left in your budget. The Iron Gym Total Upper Body Workout Bar ($30) fits most doorways and adds pull-ups, chin-ups, and hanging leg raises to your routine.
