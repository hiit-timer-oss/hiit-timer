What to Look For in HIIT Headphones

1. Secure Fit

The #1 priority for HIIT headphones is staying in your ears during burpees, jump squats, and mountain climbers. Look for ear hooks (like Powerbeats Pro) or multiple ear tip sizes for a custom fit.

2. Sweat Resistance

Minimum IPX4 rating (sweat resistant). IPX7 is even better if you sweat heavily. All our top picks are at least IPX4 rated.

3. Battery Life

You need at least 6 hours for daily workouts. Powerbeats Pro (9 hours) and Jabra Elite 4 (7 hours) outlast AirPods Pro (6 hours).

4. Sound Quality

Motivating music makes HIIT easier. Look for balanced sound with good bass for workout playlists. AirPods Pro have the best overall sound, but Powerbeats Pro have punchier bass.
